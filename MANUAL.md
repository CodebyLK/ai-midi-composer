# AI Composer Pro: User Manual

Welcome to AI Composer Pro. This tool is designed to help you quickly prototype musical ideas, edit them with precision, and export them to your favorite Digital Audio Workstation (DAW).

#### **1. Generating Music with AI**
* **Prompting:** Type a description of the music you want into the "AI Prompt" box (e.g., *A fast, aggressive bassline in D minor*).
* **Controls:** Select your desired **Length** (4, 8, or 16 bars), **Instrument**, and **BPM** (Tempo).
* **Action:** Click **✨ New**. The AI will generate a fresh composition and draw it on the Piano Roll.
* **The Arranger:** Want to make the song longer? Change your prompt, select a new length, and click **➕ Append**. The AI will generate the next section and seamlessly stitch it to the end of your current timeline.

#### **2. The Piano Roll & Draw Tools**
You don't need the AI to write music. You can manually compose directly on the grid.
* **Drawing Notes:** Select your **Draw Tool** (Single Note, Minor Triad, Major Triad) and your **Note Size** (16th note up to Whole note).
* **Adding:** Double-click anywhere on the dark grid area aligned with the piano keyboard on the left. The app will automatically calculate the music theory and stamp the note(s) down.
* **Moving:** Click and drag any existing note block to change its pitch or timing.
* **Deleting:** Right-click any note block to instantly erase it.

#### **3. Audio Recording**
Have a melody in your head? Play it on a real instrument.
1.  Click **🎤 Record Real Piano** in the left sidebar.
2.  Play your melody clearly into your microphone.
3.  Click **⏹ Stop Recording**. The app will use machine learning to analyze the audio and draw your performance as editable MIDI notes on the grid.

#### **4. The Version History (Take Manager)**
Never fear losing your work. Every time you generate a new track, record audio, or clear the grid, the app saves a snapshot of your previous workspace in the left sidebar.
* **Restore:** Click anywhere on a previous Take block to instantly load it back onto the canvas.
* **Rename:** Click the **✏️ Pencil** icon to name your takes (e.g., "Good Intro" or "Verse Idea").
* **Delete:** Click the **❌ X** icon to permanently remove a bad take to keep your workspace clean.

#### **5. Exporting Your Work**
Once your composition sounds perfect, click **⬇ Export MIDI** in the top right corner. This will download a `Piano_Session.mid` file. You can drag and drop this file into any professional software (like GarageBand, Logic Pro, FL Studio, or Ableton) to assign high-quality virtual instruments and finalize your track.
